# 1.	The modern retail landscape and the GIGO concept
## 1.1.	Context
The modern retail industry is entering a digital era where data has become ethe core asset, empowring businesses to make informed decisions and gain a competitive edge. 
The rise of Omnichannel retail models means data is generated continuously from diverse sources: Point-of-Scale (POS) systems, e-commerce platforms, loyalty programs, and inventory management systems. 
However, the rapid growth in data volume, velocity, and variety also increases the risks regarding accuracy and consistency.

In markets like Vietnam, the shift toward omnichannel is accelerating as businesses integrate data from physical stores and online platforms to build comprehensive customer profiles.
Nevertheless, data fragmentation across channels remains a significant challenge, leading to inaccuracies in demand forecasting, inventory management, and strategic planning.

## 1.2.	The GIGO concept: Garbage In, garbage out
"Garbage In, Garbage Out" (GIGO) is a fundamental principle in computer science and data analysis, emphasizing that the quality of a system's output is entirely dependent on the quality if its input. 
No matter how complex the system or how advanced the AI algorithms are, if the input data is flawed, incomplete, or biased, the resulting output will be worthless or even harmful.

Historically, this concept dates bakc to the early days of computing. Charles Babbage, the "father of the computer", once expressed surprise a the notion that a machine could produce a right answer from wrong data.
Later, in 1957, William D. Melin, a U.S. Army mathematician, reinforced this by stating that computers cannot think for themselves, and "sloppy programming" or inputs lead to incorrect results.
By the 1960s, George Fuechsel of IBM popularized the term GIGO to educate users on the critical importance off data entry accuracy.

In the world of Data Science, we do not build only models; we build trust in data. Mastering tools like Power Query or Pandas for Data Cleaning is our first line of defense against GIGO.
By transforming “Garbage” into "Gold" through rigorous cleaning and validation, we ensure that our insights drive real business value rather than costly errors.
